Paragraph Design for Web (cont.)

  • Revise your 3 inpsiring quotes. They should look clean and sharp.
  • Use fonts from Google Fonts.
  • Think again about: balance, legibility, dynamism, softness, loudness, and contrast... Play with: font size, scale, leading, alignments, contrast, margins...
  • Your HTML and CSS web files should be saved in a project folder. Label the project folder as your first name (ex: dylan – one word, all lowercase). Label the html file exactly like so: index.html. (Again, one word, all lowercase.)
  • Upload the folder to our shared google drive folder by 10:00AM next Thursday.

Paragraph Design for Print

  • Collect a quote from an acquaintance about the food options in the neighborhood they live in. You need a body of text 1000-1500 characters long.
  • Browse the example editorial layout examples in your book Designing the Editorial Experience and identity a few designs that move you the most.
  • In InDesign, lay out the quote on an 8"w x 10"h page. (Your layout may run multiple pages.) Borrow from the designs that moved you when you design your quote.
  • Think again about: balance, legibility, dynamism, softness, loudness, and contrast... Play with: font size, scale, leading, alignments, contrast, margins...
  • Use fonts from Adobe Typekit.
  • The page should have a header, a subheader that explains the assignment, your name as the designer/editor of the page, and an attribute.
  • PRINT your pages (Under Setup, toggle Scale to Fit, and under Marks and Bleed, toggle Crop Marks). Bring it to class.
  • EXPORT AS A PDF your work (in Adobe PDF (Print) format). Label the pdf your first name (ex: dylan.pdf – one word, all lowercase).
  • UPLOAD the pdf to our shared google drive folder by 10:00AM next Thursday.
