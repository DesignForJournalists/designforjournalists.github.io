Midterm Project

Scope

  • You will collaborate with a classmate to create, author, and design a print the very first issue of a zine.
  • The name of your zine is: The Newer School News.
  • Through subject matter, voice, tone, brand, and means of distribution, the zine should evoke a certain tone, personality, set of values, and perspective.
  • It must be printed in black and white ink.
  • It must include:
    - a cover (front and back)
    - a masthead
    - an editor’s letter
    - 1 front matter article
    - 1 feature article
    - 1 back-matter article
  • It can be any size, but it must be in saddle-stitch, page-spread format.
  • You can decide what the magazine is about. (What many things can “The Newer School News” mean? What do you want it to be?)
  • You can decide exactly where it is distributed, exactly when, and exactly how often. Imagine your magazine not beholden in any way by distribution or financial limitations.
  • One of you will wear the author hat. One of you will wear the designer hat. You will work together to shape the magazine.
  • When you shape the magazine, it should implicitly or explicitly evoke: who the target audience is and why the zine worth their reading? Additionally, you should clearly be able to articulate these two things.

Deliverable

  • At least 5 copies of the zine.
  • Images of the zine for me to post on the zine collection page. Each image should be under 100kb. Stylistically, here is a nice example to follow by Melissa Deckert
  • A project title, subtitle, and a 70 word description, submitted in a google document that I will share.
