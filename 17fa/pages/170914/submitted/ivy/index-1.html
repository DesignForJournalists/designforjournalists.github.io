This song from Lamar's album "good kid, m.A.A.d city" is extremely poetic, complex, and politically charged. Lamar's friend, who is about to get shot, is asking him in his final moments to promise that he will sing about his death in a postive light -- "Just promise me you'll tell the story when you make it big." His friend contemplates his life in that moment and tells Lamer that instead of getting involved in the problems he did, he wishes that he would have found a passion for something -- like Lamar did with music -- allowing him to stay out of trouble.
