“At 4pm, I’m going to fall and will need a bandaid”, said no one ever.


The idea of a first aid kit, is that it comes in handy when you least expect it. You don’t plan on using it, you just use it. The NYC First Aid Kit for Summer 2016, is an app that intends to function in that same way. it has a little bit of everything, events, kids activities, concerts, nearest beach, the article of the week and music. Just like a kit, this app is meant for everyone. Bandaids don’t discriminate, and neighter does this app. While we offer a variety of party locations, we also offer the best fishing spots for the week.

The first section of the app is “Weekly Read”. Each week we will have a new article on the summer for our app users to indulge in while on the subway or relaxing under the sun.

The Second option our users have is “Playlist for the Week”. Our music playlist is linked to Spotify, which automatically tunes the most popular songs of the moment

For parents, we offer the third section “Get Rid Of Your Kids”, with an array of camps and centers where they can drop off the kids to then go ejoy some quality time for themselves. Perfect for those moments when you call your babysitter but she’’s long gone tanning and doesn’t have time to put you first.

The last section is “Events under $10”. This section will feature concerts, beach locations, fishing locations, nerby parks and more. It will use you current location and show you, through a connection with Google Maps, the nearest spots for you on the spot.

Pulling on the our original theme of first aid, it also offers a list of nearest hospitals and call 911, for those moments when its an actual emergency. This section will also provide images on how to help someone choking or perform CPR. After all, it is a digital first aid kit.

This app is going to be your go to tool for the summer, whether you’re a mom, a young adult in roaring twenties, a beach lover, or a grandfather looking to fish and relax.

NYC First Aid Kit for Summer 2016 is an app inspired by the article Summer Survival Kit for Recession Casualties and Inflation Victims published by The New Yorker in 1970. It was created by Keilly Fernandez as her final project for Design for Jorunalist: From Typography to Interaction, a class held at The New School University and taught by Dylan Greif. Images used on the app wireframe, such as parks, beaches, or Beyonce were not taken or owned by Keilly Fernandez.
