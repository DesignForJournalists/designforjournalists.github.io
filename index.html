Course Description

Overview

What is “design for journalists”? How might this class, which combines two disciplines — graphic design and journalism — be any different studying each one as a separate class in the same semester? In this course, we will explore each discpline in the context of the other. What is the role and value of graphic design from a journalistic lense; what is the role and value of journalism from a graphic design lens; finally, how can we introduce ourselves to both discplines by strengthening our understanding of how they rely on one another in our creative and professional practices? The course is segmented in three parts:

  • 1. Foundations
  • 2. Making Meaning
  • 3. Context

1. Foundations

The first part will be a deep dive into core design principles, including typographic layout and formal composition. We will learn different ways form is can convey and provoke. Finally, we will learn the essential tools you need in your creative and professional practice. This part will entail weekly exercises.

2. Making Meaning

The second part will be about leveraging our foundations skills to communicate specific ideas and values in mind. We will also learn how to deliberately guide our audience through a user experience. Finally, we we learn how to communicate our creative ideas to other members of a creative team. This part will entail weekly assignments that build on top of each other toward a midterm project.

3. Context

Understanding core design principles and how to make meaning with them is not enough. In this final part, we we learn how understanding our audience, the time and manner of our access to them, and the circumstances in which they access us, frames the scope of our work from the very start. This part will entail weekly assignments that build on top of each other toward a final project.

Final Grading Calculation

Your grade will be based on:

  • Your completion of homework assignments on time (20%)
  • Your midterm project – timely completion, process, and product (10%)
  • Your final project – timely completion, process, and product (10%)
  • Your timely attendance and participation (20%)
  • The quality of your work over the semester (20%)
  • Your effort and growth over the semester (20%)

Attendance: Lateness and absences will affect your grade. All absences that don’t have a note from the doctor or the equivalent will be counted as unexcused. Three absences of any kind will automatically count as a fail.
