How to Break Up Like a Poet

By: Edna St. Vincent Millay

"I fell in love with her voice at once; and with her spirit, when I came to know it, so full of indomitable courage. But there was in her something of which one stood in awe — she seemed, as a poet, no mere mortal, but a goddess; and though one could not but love her, one loved her hopelessly, as a goddess must be loved. I am asking you to end a one-sided love relationship because it seems to be one-sided…. If this is true, then — I really think we can be the best of friends, and I hope you will want to, as I do. If this were a conversation, I should somewhere in the course of it, I know, ask you again to marry me. Will you … let the happiness which is possible between us come to be?"
