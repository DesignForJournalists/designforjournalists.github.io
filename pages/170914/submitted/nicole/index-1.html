The Actor's Vow

Elia Kazan was a Greek-American artist. He was a world famous producer, actor, director, and writer. Some of his most famous work includes directing the movie "A Streetcar Named Desire," directing the broadway show "Cat on a Hot Tin Roof," and writing "The Actor's Vow." "The Actor's Vow" describes the overrall goals that every actor seeks to acheive. It is a vow used to ground actors as well as inspire them to dig deeper into their craft and career.

I will raise my voice. I will be great.
