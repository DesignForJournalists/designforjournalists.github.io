Zine Project – Assignment

Overview

  • What is a zine? Check out this article by Rookie magazine about zines.
  • You will design and print the very first issue of a zine.
  • You will decide what the magazine is about.
  • You will create your zine for a specific audience in New York City (e.g. your classmates, your local park, people who like to knit...)
  • You will devise an exact time, frequency, and place you want a zine to be distributed (the subway every say, three times a year at a Friday night dinner party, a rooftop party once a month...)
  • The subject matter, articles, personality, brand, and time/frequency/place of distribution should embody a certain set of values and perspective.
  • The zine must include:
    - a cover (front and back)
    - a masthead
    - an editor’s letter
    - 1 front matter article
    - 1 feature article
    - 1 back-matter article
    If you have a very good reason, you can opt to structure your zine in a way that doesn't specifically include a front matter article, feature article, and back matter article.
  • It can be any size smaller than 9.5" height x 7.5" wide
  • The binding needs to be single-signature bound (via saddle staple, saddle stitch, or rubber band).
